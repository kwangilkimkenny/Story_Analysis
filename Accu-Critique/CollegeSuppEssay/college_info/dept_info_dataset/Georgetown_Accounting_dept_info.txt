School Name: Georgetown
Department: McDonough School of Business
Major: Accounting

Accounting majors master the technical skills essential for success in business, consulting,
government, and not-for-profit organizations. They also develop critical soft skills like teamwork,
effective business communication, and strategic thinking. The curriculum includes core courses
in financial and managerial accounting, and electives that focus on more advanced topics such as
financial statement analysis, consolidation, partnership accounting, auditing, tax accounting, and
governmental and international accounting. Accounting graduates pursue careers with a variety
of organizations, including public accounting firms, not-for-profit and government agencies,
corporations, investment banks, hedge funds, and private equity firms. The accounting major
provides an excellent foundation for graduate study in law or economics.
